living in stl guide: poverty student edition

background

this guide will give advice on how to live your best life in st. louis as a broke ass college student.

living

here are a few places for you to live off campus while not burning a whole through your wallet.

Delmar loop area

Anything that's behind United Provisions and Lofts is somewhat affordable. Enright, Cates, Interdrive. Avg unit prices 3b2b is 1900. If you get 2 other roommates your average rent is around 600$/month.

Loop North

This is further than the Enright area, but still covered by Line 5 and two shuttle lines. Avg rent around 1600, more 2b2b units and single units. If you do a 2b2b and split with another person, you get around 800$/month. Most units come with parking, sewer, trash.

Skinker-Debalievere

Honestly I don't know much about this place but people say its affordable, pricing is similiar to delmar loop but there are more robberies. also closer to campus.

Area behind Lewis park and in front of Olive

Good area, far from everything, but is alot cheaper, around 1500-1700 for a 2b2b. Sometimes you can even get close to 1k for a apartment. Is near the U City High School.

eating

you will have mealpoints, use it.

around campus
  • Vegas Wok - chinese food, 10$ after tax if you go for the braised pork rice.
  • U City Grill - 6 dollar korean food, bulgogi and bean sprouts and rice. cash only, but sadly they closed.
  • Olive grill/Ocean grill - 10 dollar chicken shawarma.
  • Chicago Chicken and Fish - 15 dollar steak dinner with baked potato and even some shrimp. But don't expect beef quality to be good.
  • Taste of India - near 20$ for a chicken tikka masala but if you eat there you have unlimited rice.
further away from campus
  • Sushi Ai - go there at noon and enjoy the 14 dollar AYCE sushi. When paying give them your student discount, whole meal is 16$ after tax and tips.
  • Joy Luck Buffet - your regular chinese buffet, 16$ after tax and tip.
  • Costco/Sam's club - hot dog + drink for a dollar fifty.

hauling your ass around

you have a u-pass, and a university shuttle system, use it.

i want two wheels

you can get a bicycle for 100$ on fb marketplace. or a electric scooter from some upperclassmen for around 80$.

i want two wheels, but faster

consider getting a 50~150cc scooter, just regular drivers license, no insurance, no plates. Avg price around 1000$. Recommended models:

  • Yamaha vino 50/125
  • Yamaha zuma 125
  • Sym super x 50/125/150
  • Honda metro (though i think its too slow)
  • Honda pcx (if you have more cash)
  • or a full on motorcycle...
i want four wheels

you can get a beater with a heater around 5000$ on fb marketplace. Insurance is gonna be probably 2k for new drivers (assuming you're an international student just like me). Also consider 10% sales tax of the purchase and PPT every year. Reg is around 50$ a year.

  • toyota corolla
  • honda civic
  • ford focus
car rentals

the school partners with enterprise car share. its 6$/hr, 60$/day, 20$ from 7pm to 8am the next day.
